Filipino Chicken Adobo

The most iconic Filipino dish, Chicken Adobo is a savory and slightly tangy braised chicken that's cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic. This comfort food is tender, flavorful, and best served with steamed rice. A true Filipino classic!

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

45 minutes

Servings

4-6 people

Difficulty

Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon whole black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Set aside.
  3. In the same pan, saut√© the minced garlic until fragrant.
  4. Return the chicken to the pan and add soy sauce, vinegar, bay leaves, and black pepper.
  5. Pour in water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  6. The chicken is done when it's tender and the sauce has reduced slightly.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt. Add brown sugar for a hint of sweetness if desired.
  8. Transfer to a serving dish and serve hot with steamed rice.

Cooking Tips

For deeper flavor, marinate the chicken in soy sauce and vinegar for 30 minutes before cooking. You can also add potatoes and hard-boiled eggs to make it more filling. The adobo tastes even better the next day after flavors have melded together. Store in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
